4WRDS

Feb - March

Details

Problem Statement

How can we design a four-wheel drive and steering system that effectively addresses the following challenges while utilising power steering?

Team size: maximum 5 members.


Goals

  • Develop a functional four-wheel steering system based on a Formula Student car (refer to Resources section).
  • Demonstrate the feasibility of integrating pneumatic power steering into a four-wheel steering system. (Bonus)
  • Address potential challenges related to space constraints, heat management, and unsprung mass.
  • Reinforcement of power transmission components to handle increased turning forces.
  • Ensure system reliability and safety through fail-safe mechanisms.
  • Explore opportunities for further optimization and potential electronic control integration.


Judging Criteria

  • Functionality (15 Points): Effectiveness, precision, and manoeuvrability gains in different modes.
  • Hydraulics (20 Points): Efficiency, packaging, heat management, reliability.
  • Mechanics (15 Points): Unsprung mass, durability, and compatibility with existing systems.
  • Power Transmission (15 Points): Handling increased forces, appropriate differential system.
  • Manufacturability & Installation (15 Points): Feasibility, ease of integration.
  • Safety & Reliability (10 Points): Fail-safe mechanisms, adherence to safety standards.
  • Cost (10 Points): Production & maintenance costs vs. benefits.


Bonus

  • Impact on vehicle performance, fuel economy, and driving experience.
  • Pneumatic system


Timeline & Submission Details

  • Report: Submit a detailed written report of 8 pages (max.) in PDF format, including:
    • Design concept and schematics.
    • Engineering calculations and analysis.
    • Component specifications and materials selection.
    • Manufacturing and installation considerations.
    • Safety and reliability measures.
  • Related files: Kindly upload the CAD files and a .mp4 animation to either Google Drive or OneDrive, and subsequently include the corresponding link in the accompanying report.
  • Bill of Materials: Provide a 1-pager document (max.) in PDF format listing out the estimated cost of equipment and manufacturing.
  • Deadline : 24th March'24
  • Presentation
    • Duration: 10 minutes
    • Format: maximum 5 slides, excluding cover and thank you slide.
