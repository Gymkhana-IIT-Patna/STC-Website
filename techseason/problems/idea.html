VortexBot

Feb-March

Details

Problem statement

Building advanced bots can be prohibitively expensive due to the need for specialised hardware and software. However, a cost-effective approach leverages smartphone sensors and processing power for bot control through computer vision. Harnessing the capabilities of modern smartphones reduces development costs, making sophisticated bot interactions more accessible and affordable for a wider range of applications.

Build an autonomous four-wheeled bot which uses an android smartphone and its sensors like camera, gyroscope, accelerometer, etc. The bot should process the inputs and perform the following tasks:
  • Line following
  • Person following
  • Traffic signs detection


Judging Criteria

The evaluation will be on the basis of:

  • Line following: 20
  • Person following: 20
  • Traffic signs detection: 20
  • Application development: 20
  • Performance: 10
  • Originality: 10


Rules

  • Report:
    • Should not be of more than 25 pages
    • Must include problem formulation, methodology, and approach.
    • Have clear illustrations and explanations of algorithms involved.
  • Code:
    • Needs to be submitted in the form of GitHub repository.
    • Must be well-organized, commented, and documented.
  • Prototype:
    • No external sensors are allowed in the bot.
    • All tasks need to be performed using the phone camera.
    • The bot should be fully autonomous and must not be controlled using any kind of external controller.
    • No motor driver/microcontroller other than the provided ones must be used.
    • Judges can ask to explain the functioning of the bot during the demonstrations.
  • Plagiarism is strictly prohibited.


Other Details

  • Submissions and their respective deadlines are as follows:
    • PDF Report: 25th February
    • Presentation: 3rd March
    • Prototype: 3rd March
  • Resources:
    • Arduino Android Serial Communication
    • TensorFlow Lite for Android
